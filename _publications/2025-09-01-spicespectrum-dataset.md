This data paper introduces SpiceSpectrum, a comprehensive visual dataset of 11 globally significant spices comprising 11,000 high-quality images curated to support research and applications in agriculture, trade, and culinary arts.

**Dataset Specifications:**
- **11 Spice Classes**: Black Pepper, Cardamom, Cinnamon, Cloves, Coriander, Cumin, Ginger, Nutmeg, Paprika, Saffron, and Turmeric
- **11,000 images total** (1,000 images per spice class)
- **Resolution**: Standardized 512 Ã— 512 pixels
- **Class-balanced**: Perfect balance with exactly 1,000 images per class

**Key Features:**
- Selected spices based on economic importance, medicinal value, and widespread culinary use across global cuisines
- Diverse real-world scenarios including:
  - Multiple lighting conditions
  - Various backgrounds
  - Natural textures
  - Different packaging styles
  - Multiple capture angles
- Organized into dedicated folders for ease of use
- High consistency in image quality and standardization

**Applications:**
- Machine learning and deep learning model training
- Computer vision research
- Agricultural classification systems
- Quality control in spice trade
- Educational resources for culinary arts
- Food industry automation

**Key Contributions:**
- Contributed to dataset conceptualization and validation
- Ensured real-world diversity in data collection methodology
- Maintained standardization protocols for image quality
- Provided organized structure for immediate research use

This dataset addresses the need for high-quality, standardized visual data of commercially valuable spices and serves as a valuable resource for both academic research and practical applications in agriculture, trade, and the food industry.
